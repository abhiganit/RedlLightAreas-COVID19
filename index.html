Impact of Red Light Area on Covid-19 in India

To combat COVID-19 epidemic in India, the Indian government implemented a three-week widespread national lockdown beginning on March 24, 2020. The lockdown was subsequently extended thrice and now ends on May 31. By implementing an early comprehensive lockdown, India has so far avoided overwhelming its healthcare resources. However, as officials now cautiously begin to lift restrictions, it is crucial to identify geographic hot spots that may disproportionately exacerbate the spread of COVID-19. In this study, we show that red-light areas in India are one such place.

We developed a dynamical mathematical model where we modeled COVID-19 transmission nationally and in 5 major cities of India and red-light areas within them. We first implemented a 68 days lockdown (March 24-May 31) in our model, effectively quarantining everyone. We considered two scenarios after June 1:

  • When red-light area reopen from June 1 onwards
  • Red-light area closure is continued.

We compared cumulative cases, hospitalizations and deaths at the peak of epidemic under the scenario of re-opening with the corresponding numbers at that time if red-light area remained close.

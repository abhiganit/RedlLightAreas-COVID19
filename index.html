Impact of Red Light Area on Covid-19 in India

1 Introduction

2 Methods

We developed an age-structured SEIR-type dynamic model for COVID-19 transmission to understand the impact of continued closure of red light areas in five cities of India as well as nationally after the initial countrywide lockdown of 40 days from March 24,2020 to May 3, 2020 (Figure 1). The population of each location considered is divided into red light areas and general population. Red light area includes sex workers as well as non-sex workers such as pimps, brothel managers, security, servants and other performing miscellaneous roles in the area. General population is rest of the location's population. Each population is further stratified into four age-groups and compartments (Table 2) based on natural history of disease as well as interventions. For population stratification into four age groups: 0-19, 20-39, 50-64 and 65+ years of age, we apply age-distribution of each location from last census data (Census2011) to current population estimates for each location and red light areas within it (Table 1). After a susceptible individual (\(S\)) acquires infection, they remain in incubation period (\(E\)) for an average of \(1/\sigma = 5.2\) days (Table 3). We assume that a person is not infectious during their incubation period. Following incubation period, an infected individual either remains asymptomatic (\(I_A\)) or develops symptoms (\(I_N\),\(I_H\)). A proportion of symptomatic individuals (\((1-h)\)) only develop mild symptoms (\(I_N\)). Symptomatic individuals with mild symptoms (\(I_N\),\(Q_N\)) do not need hospitalization and recover on an average of \(1/\gamma = 4.6\) days (Table 3). A proportion of individuals (\(f = 0.05\)) with mild or severe symptoms are isolated within a day (\(I_N\)->\(Q_N\),\(I_H\)->\(Q_H\)). Symptomatic individuals with severe symptoms (\(I_H\),\(Q_H\)) either need just hospitalization(\(H\)) or also need to be in ICU (\(C\)). Those hospitalized (\(H\),\(C\)) either recover or die. Prem et. al estimated contact patterns between different age-groups in India overall and by different locations such as household. We use this estimate of overall contact pattern in India to inform the contact mixing between different age-groups in our model. Contact mixing of individuals who are isolated (\(Q_N\),\(Q_H\)) is informed by contact patterns for household.

Figure 1: Model Schematic

The force of infection \(\lambda\) is given by

\begin{equation} \lambda = \frac{B C_{M} [ M_{A}(k_{A} I_{A}+k_{M} I_{N}+I_{H})+M_{H}(k_{M}Q_{N}+Q_{H})]}{P} \end{equation}

where \(B\) represents matrix representing probability of infection within and between a location and red light area within it. Interaction between general population and red light area occurs through customers from general population and given an interaction, probability of infection for interaction between general population and red light area is assumed to be 1. Furthermore, we assume that probability of infection within red light area is same as the probability of infection in general population (\(\beta\)), which is calibrated to basic reproduction number \(R_0\). Thus \(B\) is given by

\begin{equation} B = \begin{bmatrix} \beta & 1 \\ 1 & \beta \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}

The interaction between general population and red light area is informed in our model through a connectivity matrix \(C_M\), where contact rate \(c_r\) (Table 1) between the general population and red light area is calculated as a ratio of daily total interaction of general population with red light area and total population of the location (Table 1). The connectivity matrix \(C_M\) is therefore given by

\begin{equation} C_M = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & c_r \\ c_r & 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}

Individuals with mild infections are only 50% infectious compared to the severe infections and we assume the relative infectiousness of asymptomatic cases to be the same (Table 3). The contact pattern between different age-groups are informed by matrices \(M_A\) and \(M_H\) respectively (Prem et. al.). \(M_A\) represents the overall contact patterns and if individuals are isolated/quarantined in their home (\(Q_N\),\(Q_H\)), we use \(M_H\) matrix that represents the contact patterns at household level.

2.1 Implementation of 40 days national lockdown

To implement 40 days national lockdown in our model, we assume that everyone remains at home and their contact patterns are informed by the household matrix \(M_H\) for the duration of lockdown. Moreover, as there is no interaction between general population and red light area during the lockdown, we set the contact rate \(c_r = 0\).

2.2 Post lockdown

After the initial lockdown period, contact patterns are informed by the overall contact matrix \(M_A\) and it is assumed that as a result of improved contact tracing capacity achieved during lockdown, 50% of symptomatic cases are isolated after lockdown period(Mandal et. al.). For scenario of continued closure of red light area after lockdown, we keep the contact rate \(c_r = 0\), else it is set back to its original value (Table 1).
